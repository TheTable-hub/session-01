Before you pull up a chair...

This guide is not homework. It is an invitation. Spend some time with the passages below before we gather. Let them sit with you. Let them ask their questions. You do not need to arrive with answers — you need to arrive with an honest curiosity and a willingness to follow the evidence wherever it leads.

Bring your Bible, bring your questions, and bring yourself. That is enough.

Step 1 — Read
1
John 8:48–59

Before Abraham was — I am.

Read the full exchange between Jesus and the religious leaders. Pay attention to what triggers their reaction at the end of the passage. It is not what most people expect.

2
John 10:22–39

I and the Father are one.

A short, loaded passage. Read the crowd's response carefully — verse 33 especially. They say something very specific about what they understood Jesus to mean.

3
Mark 2:1–12

Your sins are forgiven.

A paralysed man. A crowded room. Four friends with enormous faith. And then four words from Jesus that change everything — long before the healing happens.

4
John 20:24–29

My Lord and my God.

Thomas — the great doubter — has the most direct encounter of all. Read what he says, and notice what Jesus does not say in response. That silence is the point.

Step 2 — Background
What This Session Is About

The question that divides the world.

Jesus is the most talked-about, written-about, argued-about figure in all of human history. Almost every major faith tradition has something to say about him. Most agree he was real. Many agree he was remarkable. The dividing question is the one he himself forced: was he simply a great teacher and chosen messenger — or was he something else entirely?

In the first century, Jewish theology had a clear category for someone like Jesus: the Messiah — God's anointed representative. A human being, chosen and sent. Not God himself. So the question we need to sit with is this: did Jesus fit into that category — or did he break it? We will examine four moments where he seems to have broken it deliberately. Come ready to engage them honestly.

Step 3 — Journal
Question 1

"Why do you think the question of who Jesus is still matters today — two thousand years later?"

There is no right answer here. Write what you actually think, not what you think you should think.

Question 2

"What is the difference between admiring Jesus as a great teacher and actually following him as Lord?"

Think about what each option costs. And what each one gives.

Question 3

"If Jesus really is God — not just a good man — how does that change things for you personally?"

Be honest. This is the question the whole session is building toward.

Step 4 — Come Ready

Bring your Bible

Any version you are comfortable reading from. We will be moving through the passages together at the session.

Two hours together

We start and end on time. The session flows through story, structure, discussion, and a closing reflection.

No pressure

You are not expected to have answers. You are expected to be present. Questions are welcome — even the uncomfortable ones.

All ages welcome

Teenagers, young adults, and parents sit together. Different perspectives make the discussion richer for everyone.

A Prayer Before You Come

"Lord, open my eyes to see what is true, open my mind to follow the evidence honestly, and open my heart to respond to whatever I find. Amen."
