Before you pull up a chair...

This is one of the shortest psalms in the entire Bible — only eight verses. But do not let its length fool you. Psalm 3 was written in the darkest moment of one of the greatest lives ever lived, and it carries a weight that goes far beyond its size.

Read it slowly. More than once if you can. Let the questions sit with you through the week before we gather. You do not need to arrive with answers — you need to arrive with an honest heart. That is enough.

Step 1 — The Psalm
Psalm 3 — A Psalm of David, when he fled from Absalom his son
Verses 1–2

Lord, how many are my foes. How many rise up against me. Many are saying of me, "God will not deliver him."

Verses 3–4

"But you, Lord, are a shield around me, my glory, the One who lifts my head high. I call out to the Lord, and he answers me from his holy mountain."

Verses 5–6

I lie down and sleep. I wake again, because the Lord sustains me. I will not fear though tens of thousands assail me on every side.

Verses 7–8

Arise, Lord. Deliver me, my God. Strike all my enemies on the jaw. Break the teeth of the wicked. From the Lord comes deliverance. May your blessing be on your people.

Step 2 — Background
What This Session Is About

A king on the run. A psalm that endures.

David wrote Psalm 3 at one of the lowest points of his entire life. His son Absalom had launched a full rebellion against him — raised an army, seized Jerusalem, and was actively hunting his own father. David's closest friends had abandoned him and joined the rebellion. And people were saying the worst thing imaginable: that God had given up on David entirely.

And yet — in the middle of all of that — David slept. This psalm is the record of how a man in that much pain found that much peace. And the answer is not a technique or a formula. It is a person. Two words at the heart of the psalm tell us everything: But you.

Step 3 — Three Movements
Movement One · Verses 1–2

The Cry

David begins not with praise but with brutal honesty. He names the enemies, counts the betrayals, and repeats the accusation that God has abandoned him. This psalm teaches us that honest prayer is not a lack of faith — it is the beginning of it.

"Lord, how many are my foes. How many rise up against me."
Movement Two · Verses 3–4

The Shield

Two words change everything. David stops looking at what is against him and declares who is for him. He does not argue with the voices. He turns away from the noise and speaks directly to God — making a declaration of fact, not a desperate plea.

"But you, Lord, are a shield around me, my glory, the One who lifts my head high."
Movement Three · Verses 5–8

The Rest

The proof that the declaration was real. David sleeps — not because the danger has passed, but because he has genuinely handed it to someone bigger than himself. And he wakes not just surviving but declaring victory.

"I lie down and sleep. I wake again, because the Lord sustains me."
Step 4 — Journal
Question 1

"What in your life right now feels like tens of thousands arrayed against you?"

Be specific. Do not write what you think you should feel — write what you actually feel.
Question 2

"What would it look like for you to say — genuinely, not just with your lips — 'But you, Lord'?"

There is a difference between saying the right words and actually turning your gaze. What would turning your gaze look like for you this week?
Question 3

"Is there something you are carrying that God is asking you to hand over and finally, actually, rest?"

The psalm ends with salvation belonging to the Lord — not to us. What are you still trying to carry that was never yours to carry?
Step 5 — Come Ready

Bring your Bible

Any version you are comfortable reading from. We will walk through all eight verses together as a group.

4–6pm on Sunday

Two hours together. We start and end on time. Come settled and ready to engage.

Ages 13 and above

Teenagers, young adults, and parents are all welcome. Every voice around the table matters.

No pressure

Questions are welcome. Doubts are welcome. You do not need to have this figured out before you arrive.

A Prayer Before You Come

"Lord, wherever I am carrying something too heavy for me, help me to find the courage to say — but you, Lord. And then help me to actually let go. Amen."
